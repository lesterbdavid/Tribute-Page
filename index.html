Miriam Defensor Santiago

The Iron Lady of Asia

Here's a timeline of Miriam Defensor Santiago

  • 1945 - Born in Iloilo City and was the eldest of seven children
  • 1965 - Defensor Santiago graduated magna cum laude from the University of the Philippines Visayas with a Bachelor of Art Degree in political science
  • 1988 - After martial law, she was appointed as a commissioner of the Bureau of Immigration and Deportation
  • 1991 - underwents surgery on the jaw, after getting severely injured in car crash while campaigning, and at one point she has administered her last rites by a Catholic priest. She claimed the incident as an assassination attempt. Two months later, she was back on the campaign trail
  • 1992 - Defensor Santiago has been dubbed as "The Iron Lady of Asia" and the "Dragon Lady" due to her scathing but bold eloquence both in leadership and writing
  • 1995 - Defensor Santiago was first elected senator
  • 1997 - rans for president, she won and preserved the people's mandate for term limits
  • 1998 - rans again for president, but lost amidst heavy propaganda concerning her mental health, which was later proven false
  • 2001 - losts re-election to the Senate
  • 2004 - rans for senator and was elected. Defensor Santiago focused on creating significant laws that changed the country as a whole
  • 2010 - rans again for senator and won. Serves as a chair of the Foreign Affairs Comittee of the Commission on Appointments. She exposed illegal gambling lords and illegal-logging lords throughout her terms
  • 2011 - was the first Asian from a third-world country to be elected as a judge of the International Criminal Court (ICC)
  • 2014 - Santiago announces her current health condition that she was diagnosed with stage 4 lung cancer. However, she said that her cancer is not metastasic and is expected to be cured within six weeks through molecularly targeted therapy
  • 2015 - announces her intention to run in the 2016 Philippine presidential election after her cancer was deemed 'stable' and 'receded' by doctors from the United States
  • 2016 - dies at the age of 71 years-old
